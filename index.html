Fast Break to Las Vegas: NBA Relocation Toolkit

By CT Taber

1. Introduction

This capstone project investigates the economic and fan engagement implications of relocating an NBA franchise to Las Vegas. With growing interest in expansion and strategic market shifts, this toolkit combines data analytics, predictive modeling, and fan sentiment to inform relocation decisions.

2. Background & Motivation

Las Vegas has emerged as a leading candidate for an NBA franchise due to its tourism economy, population growth, and success in other sports markets (Raiders, Golden Knights, Aces). This project is fueled by a passion for basketball, analytics, and front-office strategy.

3. Methodology / Approach

  • Tools used: Python, Pandas, Plotly, Streamlit, Canva, Excel
  • NBA datasets analyzed from Kaggle, Basketball Reference
  • Key components: fan base analysis, relocation history, team value growth, expansion case studies
  • Custom Streamlit dashboard built for interactive visualization and storytelling

4. Results & Findings

Our dashboard visualizes shot chart trends, city comparisons, and Vegas expansion case logic. Key findings include:

  • Las Vegas ranks high in growth potential and tourism revenue
  • NBA relocation historically results in franchise value increases
  • Fan sentiment and branding continuity are critical factors

Launch Streamlit Dashboard

5. Discussion & Future Work

This project taught me how data can drive sports business decisions. Limitations include limited access to proprietary NBA financial data and the challenge of modeling fan loyalty. Future work could explore a full relocation simulator or real-time fan polling during NBA seasons.
